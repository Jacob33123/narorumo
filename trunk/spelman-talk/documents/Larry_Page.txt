*Lawrence Edward "Larry" Page* (born March 26, 1973) is an American
</wiki/United_States> entrepreneur </wiki/Entrepreneur> who co-founded
the Google </wiki/Google_search> web search engine
</wiki/Web_search_engine>, now Google Inc. </wiki/Google>, with Sergey
Brin </wiki/Sergey_Brin>.^[4] <#cite_note-3>

    [edit </w/index.php?title=Larry_Page&action=edit&section=1>] Biography


      [edit </w/index.php?title=Larry_Page&action=edit&section=2>] Early
      life and education

Larry Page is the son of the late Dr. Carl Victor Page, a professor of
computer science and artificial intelligence
</wiki/Artificial_intelligence> at Michigan State University
</wiki/Michigan_State_University> and one of the University of Michigan
</wiki/University_of_Michigan>'s first computer science Ph.D graduates,
and Gloria Page, a computer programming teacher at Michigan State
University </wiki/Michigan_State_University>. Despite his mother being
Jewish </wiki/Jewish>, Page was raised similarly to his father: without
a religion.^[5] <#cite_note-4> ^[6] <#cite_note-5> He is also the
brother of Carl Victor Page, Jr.,^[7] <#cite_note-6> a co-founder of
eGroups </wiki/EGroups>, later sold to Yahoo! </wiki/Yahoo!> for
approximately half a billion dollars.

Page attended a Montessori </wiki/Montessori> school in Lansing,
Michigan </wiki/Lansing,_Michigan>, and graduated from East Lansing High
School </wiki/East_Lansing_High_School>. Page holds a Bachelor of
Science </wiki/Bachelor_of_Science> degree </wiki/Academic_degree> in
computer engineering </wiki/Computer_engineering> from the University of
Michigan </wiki/University_of_Michigan> with honors and a Masters degree
</wiki/Masters_degree> in Computer Science </wiki/Computer_Science> from
Stanford University </wiki/Stanford_University>. While at the University
of Michigan, "Page created an inkjet printer </wiki/Inkjet_printer>,
made of Lego </wiki/Lego> bricks"^[8] <#cite_note-corpexecs-7> , was a
member of the solar car team
</wiki/1993_%22Maize_%26_Blue%22_University_of_Michigan_Solar_Car_Team>
and served as the president of the HKN </wiki/HKN>.^[9] <#cite_note-8>


      [edit </w/index.php?title=Larry_Page&action=edit&section=3>] Research

After enrolling for a Ph.D </wiki/Ph.D>. program in computer science at
Stanford University </wiki/Stanford_University>, Page was in search for
a dissertation theme and considered - among other things - exploring the
mathematical properties of the World Wide Web </wiki/World_Wide_Web>,
understanding its link structure as a huge graph
</wiki/Graph_(mathematics)>.^[10] <#cite_note-wiredbirth-9> His
supervisor Terry Winograd </wiki/Terry_Winograd> encouraged him to
pursue this idea (which Page later recalled as "the best advice I ever
got"^[11] <#cite_note-10> ) and Page focused on the problem of finding
out which web pages link to a given page, considering the number and
nature of such backlinks </wiki/Backlink> to be valuable information
</wiki/Information> about that page (with the role of citations
</wiki/Citation> in academic publishing </wiki/Academic_publishing> in
mind).^[10] <#cite_note-wiredbirth-9> In his research project, nicknamed
"BackRub," he was soon joined by Sergey Brin </wiki/Sergey_Brin>, a
fellow Stanford Ph.D. student and close friend, whom he had first met in
the summer of 1995 in a group of potential new students which Brin had
volunteered to show around the campus.^[10] <#cite_note-wiredbirth-9> To
convert the backlink data gathered by BackRub's web crawler
</wiki/Web_crawler> into a measure of importance for a given web page,
Brin and Page developed the PageRank </wiki/PageRank> algorithm, and
realized that it could be used to build a search engine
</wiki/Search_engine_(computing)> far superior to existing ones.^[10]
<#cite_note-wiredbirth-9> In August 1996 the initial version of Google
was made available, still on the Stanford University Web site.^[10]
<#cite_note-wiredbirth-9>


      [edit </w/index.php?title=Larry_Page&action=edit&section=4>] Business

In 1998, Brin and Page founded Google, Inc. Page is still "on leave"
from the Ph.D. program.^[12] <#cite_note-11>

Page ran Google as co-president with Brin until 2001 when they hired
Eric Schmidt </wiki/Eric_E._Schmidt> to become Chairman and CEO of Google.

According to the 2007 edition of Forbes </wiki/Forbes>, Page had an
estimated net worth of $ </wiki/United_States_dollar>16.6 Billion,
placing him at rank 26 on Forbes's list of the richest persons in the
world, together with Brin.^[13] <#cite_note-12> Page and Brin
</wiki/Sergey_Brin> recently purchased a pre-owned Qantas Boeing 767
</wiki/Boeing_767> airliner for their business and personal needs.

In 2007, Page was cited by PC World </wiki/PC_World_(magazine)> as #1 on
the list of the 50 most important people on the web, along with Brin and
Schmidt.^[14] <#cite_note-13>

Page is also an investor in Tesla Motors </wiki/Tesla_Motors>, which
developed the Tesla Roadster </wiki/Tesla_Roadster>, a 220-mile (350 km)
range battery electric vehicle </wiki/Battery_electric_vehicle>.^[15]
<#cite_note-14>
